Extend the existing "AI & Workforce Intelligence – Mother Dashboard"
by adding a NEW SECTION called:

"Describe Your Role"

This section allows users to describe their own role in plain language
(no CV upload, no personal data storage).

The goal is to:
- map a user-described role to task categories
- show task-level AI interaction:
  Augmented vs Automated vs Stable Human
- follow a logic similar to Germany’s Job-Futuromat,
  but adapted to AI augmentation (not only automation)

This is a LOW-RISK, EXPLORATORY FEATURE.
Use mock logic and transparent assumptions.

========================
SECTION PURPOSE
========================
Help users understand:
- how AI may interact with the tasks they perform
- which parts of their work are more likely to be supported, automated, or remain human
- without predicting job loss or employability

========================
UI DESIGN
========================

Add a new section BELOW the main dashboard:

### Section Title
"Describe Your Role"

Subtitle:
"Describe what you do. We analyze task patterns, not people."

Small disclaimer:
"This is an interpretive tool based on research patterns.
It does not predict individual outcomes."

------------------------
INPUT COMPONENTS
------------------------

1) Role Description Input
- Large text area
- Placeholder text:
  "Describe your role and main tasks.
   Example: managing programs, coordinating stakeholders,
   writing reports, tracking budgets, planning timelines..."

2) Sector Selector (required)
- Dropdown:
  ["Health", "IT", "Logistics", "Administration", "Education", "Other"]

3) Role Context Selector
- Radio buttons:
  ["Current role", "Previous role"]

4) Optional: Task Emphasis Slider (optional, mock)
- Slider with labels:
  "More coordination ←→ More technical"

------------------------
PROCESSING LOGIC (MOCK)
------------------------

When the user clicks "Analyze role":

1) Extract task signals from the text:
   - coordination & communication
   - analysis & planning
   - documentation & reporting
   - operational execution
   - people management
   - creative / judgment-based tasks

2) Map extracted task categories to
   mock AI task interaction patterns:

   Example mapping logic (simplified):
   - Documentation & reporting → Mostly augmented
   - Routine coordination → Partially automated
   - Analysis & planning → Augmented
   - People management & judgment → Mostly human
   - Creative / strategic decisions → Human-dominant

3) Combine with selected sector to slightly adjust exposure
   (e.g. health vs admin vs IT)

All mappings must be explainable and transparent.
