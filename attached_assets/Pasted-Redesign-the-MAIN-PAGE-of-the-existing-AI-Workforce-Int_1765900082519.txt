Redesign the MAIN PAGE of the existing
"AI & Workforce Intelligence Dashboard"
to prioritize CLARITY, USER VALUE, and SENSE-MAKING.

This is NOT a KPI dashboard.
It is a GUIDED EXPLANATION interface.

Use existing mock indicators and data,
but change the STRUCTURE, HIERARCHY, and COPY.

================================
CORE GOAL
================================
In under 2 minutes, the user should understand:

1) What kind of AI-driven change is happening in this role/context
2) Why this change is happening (at task level)
3) What this means for work and hiring — and what it does NOT mean

================================
DESIGN PRINCIPLES
================================
- Fewer charts, more meaning
- One clear takeaway above the fold
- Charts support the story, not replace it
- Calm, institutional tone (OECD / IAB style)
- Explicit interpretation (no guessing by the user)

================================
NEW MAIN PAGE STRUCTURE
================================

--------------------------------
SECTION 1 — BIG PICTURE INSIGHT (ABOVE THE FOLD)
--------------------------------

Replace the current summary strip with ONE central insight card.

Title (large, human language):
"AI is mainly supporting this role — not replacing it"

Subtitle (1–2 lines):
"Most changes affect how tasks are done, not whether the role exists."

Below the subtitle, show THREE compact signals (icons or pills):

- Task impact: Mostly augmenting
- Skill change: Moderate
- Demand context: Growing

These are labels, not scores.

Small disclaimer text below:
"These are structural patterns, not individual predictions."

This section must be visually dominant and immediately understandable.

--------------------------------
SECTION 2 — WHY THIS IS HAPPENING (TASK STORY FIRST)
--------------------------------

Title:
"Why this role is changing"

Layout: two columns

LEFT (TEXT — PRIMARY):
Short explanatory paragraph, example tone:
"In this role, AI is mainly used to support documentation, coordination,
and analytical preparation. Core responsibilities such as decision-making,
accountability, and stakeholder trust remain human."

RIGHT (VISUAL — SECONDARY):
- Simplified task transformation chart
  (Augmented / Automated / Stable Human)
- Remove any 'Total' bar
- Minimal labels

Tooltip:
"AI affects tasks within roles, not entire jobs."

--------------------------------
SECTION 3 — WHAT 'MOSTLY AUGMENTING' MEANS IN PRACTICE
--------------------------------

Dedicated explanation card (IMPORTANT).

Title:
"What ‘mostly augmenting’ means in practice"

Exactly THREE bullets:
- AI reduces time spent on routine and support tasks
- Expectations and scope often increase, not free time
- Hiring tends to favor experienced or adaptable profiles

Explicit sentence at bottom:
"Augmentation rarely means one person replaces two people."

--------------------------------
SECTION 4 — SKILLS & DEMAND CONTEXT (MERGED VIEW)
--------------------------------

Title:
"Skills and labor demand context"

Layout: compact and calm.

Include:
- Small skill change trend (visual or label)
- Small labor demand signal
- One synthesis sentence tying them together, example:
"Skills are evolving at a moderate pace, while demand remains strong —
suggesting adaptation pressure rather than role decline."

No separate charts unless necessary.

--------------------------------
SECTION 5 — OPTIONAL DEEP DIVE (PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE)
--------------------------------

Collapsed by default.

Label:
"Explore details"

Inside this section:
- Sector comparison
- Augmentation vs automation scale
- Integration context (language / credential sensitivity)

These are secondary and should not distract from the main message.

--------------------------------
SECTION 6 — INTERPRETATION & LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------

Title:
"What this means — and what it doesn’t"

Include:
- 3 bullets: what the patterns suggest
- 2 bullets: what they do NOT mean
- Short paragraph on uncertainty and context-dependence

This section is mandatory and clearly separated from charts.
