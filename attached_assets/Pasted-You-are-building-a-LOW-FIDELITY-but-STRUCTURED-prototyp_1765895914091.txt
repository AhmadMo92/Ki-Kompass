This is NOT:
- a job board

This IS:
- a research-inspired dashboard
- focused on tasks, skills, and labor market structure
- calm, non-alarmist, explanatory

---

## CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

- Task- and skill-based framing (not job loss)
- Research / OECD / IAB tone
- Clear separation between:
  DATA → INTERPRETATION → LIMITATIONS
- Designed for general workforce + migrants
- No scores, rankings, or red/green danger signals

---

## DASHBOARD GOAL

In 3–5 minutes, the user should understand:
1) How AI interacts with tasks
2) How fast skills are changing
3) Where labor market demand exists
4) Where change pressure and opportunity coexist
5) What this DOES and DOES NOT mean

---

## REQUIRED INDICATORS (8 TOTAL)

Use SAMPLE DATA only.

1) Task AI Exposure
   - % Augmented tasks
   - % Partially automated tasks
   - % Mostly stable tasks

2) Augmentation vs Automation Balance
   - Qualitative label: Mostly Augmenting / Mixed / Mostly Automating

3) Skill Change Intensity
   - Low / Medium / High
   - Display as a simple time trend

4) Sector AI Exposure
   - Relative exposure by sector (bar chart)

5) Labor Demand Signal
   - Growing / Stable / Declining
   - By sector or role group

6) Opportunity Alignment
   - Overlap between:
     - skill profile
     - augmentation-heavy tasks
     - growing demand sectors

7) Language Sensitivity (Context Indicator)
   - Low / Medium / High

8) Credential Dependency (Context Indicator)
   - Low / Medium / High

## PAGE STRUCTURE

### 1. HEADER
Title:
"AI, Work, and Skill Transformation"

Subtitle:
"A task-based, research-backed view of how AI reshapes work"

Small disclaimer text:
"This dashboard explains structural patterns and trends.
It does not predict individual outcomes."

---

### 2. FILTER BAR (INTERACTIVE)

Include dropdowns for:
- Sector (e.g. Health, IT, Logistics, Administration)
- Broad Role Group (e.g. Analytical, Operational, Service)
- Region (placeholder)

Optional toggle:
- "Show integration context"

Changing filters updates ALL indicators.

---

### 3. SUMMARY INSIGHT STRIP (TOP)

A dynamic text summary that updates based on filters.

Example tone:
"In this context, AI is mainly augmenting tasks.
Skill requirements are changing at a moderate pace.
Labor demand remains strong in adjacent roles."

This must be TEXT — not a score.

---

### 4. MAIN DASHBOARD PANELS

#### PANEL A — TASK TRANSFORMATION
- Stacked bar or donut chart
  - Augmented
  - Automated
  - Stable tasks
- Tooltip:
  "AI affects tasks within jobs, not entire occupations."

---

#### PANEL B — AUGMENTATION VS AUTOMATION
- Simple qualitative indicator
- Short explanation text:
  "This reflects how AI is typically used in this context."

---

#### PANEL C — SKILL CHANGE DYNAMICS
- Line or band chart over time
- Caption:
  "This shows how quickly skill requirements evolve,
   not the risk of job loss."

---

#### PANEL D — SECTOR AI EXPOSURE
- Bar chart by sector
- Neutral colors only

---

#### PANEL E — LABOR DEMAND SIGNAL
- Simple labels or small bars
- Caption:
  "Demand reflects current labor market conditions."

---

#### PANEL F — OPPORTUNITY ALIGNMENT
- Simple visual or list
- Show overlap between:
  skills + augmentation + demand
- Caption:
  "Structural opportunity areas, not guarantees."

---

#### PANEL G — CONTEXT & ACCESS (TOGGLEABLE)

Visible only if "Show integration context" is ON.

Show:
- Language Sensitivity
- Credential Dependency

Caption:
"Access to opportunities depends on language,
credential recognition, and institutional context."

---

### 5. INTERPRETATION PANEL (MANDATORY)

Title:
"What this means — and what it doesn't"

Include:
- 3 bullets: What the indicators suggest
- 2 bullets: What they do NOT mean
- Short paragraph on uncertainty and assumptions
