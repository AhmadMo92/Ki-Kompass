You are building a LOW-FIDELITY prototype of an interactive
"AI & Workforce Intelligence Dashboard".

This prototype is for DESIGN EXPLORATION ONLY.
Indicators are placeholders but must be conceptually correct.

The platform explains how AI reshapes work through TASKS, SKILLS,
and LABOR MARKET CONTEXT — not job loss or individual predictions.

### CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES
- Research-backed tone (OECD / IAB style)
- Calm, non-alarmist language
- Tasks and skills, not jobs disappearing
- Clear separation between:
  data → interpretation → uncertainty
- Suitable for a general workforce audience, including migrants

---

## DASHBOARD GOAL

Help a user understand in 3–5 minutes:
1) How AI interacts with tasks in their context
2) How skill requirements are changing
3) Where labor market opportunities exist
4) What this DOES and DOES NOT mean

---

## REQUIRED INDICATORS (USE PLACEHOLDER DATA)

Implement the following conceptual indicators:

1. Task AI Exposure
   - % of tasks likely to be augmented
   - % of tasks potentially automated
   - % of tasks largely stable

2. Augmentation vs Automation Balance
   - Qualitative indicator: "Mostly Augmenting / Mixed / Mostly Automating"

3. Skill Change Intensity
   - Low / Medium / High (time-based trend)

4. Sector AI Exposure
   - Relative exposure by sector (bar chart)

5. Labor Demand Signal
   - Stable / Growing / Declining (by sector or role group)

6. Opportunity Alignment
   - Overlap between:
     - skill profile
     - growing demand sectors
     - augmentation-heavy tasks

7. Context Sensitivity (optional toggle)
   - Language dependency: Low / Medium / High
   - Credential dependency: Low / Medium / High

  "This platform explains structural patterns and trends.

---

### 2. FILTER BAR (INTERACTIVE)
Allow user to select:
- Sector (dropdown)
- Broad role group (dropdown)
- Region (optional placeholder)
- Toggle: "Show integration context"

Changing filters updates all indicators.

---

### 3. SUMMARY INSIGHT STRIP (TOP)
Dynamic text summary that updates based on filters.

Example structure:
"In this context, AI is mainly augmenting tasks.
Skill requirements are changing at a moderate pace.
Labor demand remains strong in adjacent roles."

This is TEXT, not a score.

### 4. MAIN DASHBOARD PANELS

#### PANEL A — TASK TRANSFORMATION
- Stacked bar or donut chart:
  - Augmented tasks
  - Automated tasks
  - Stable human tasks
- Tooltip:
  "AI affects tasks within jobs, not entire occupations."

---

#### PANEL B — SKILL CHANGE DYNAMICS
- Line or band chart showing Skill Change Intensity over time
- Explanatory caption:
  "This reflects how quickly skill requirements evolve,
   not the risk of job loss."

---

#### PANEL C — SECTOR CONTEXT
- Bar chart of AI exposure by sector
- Neutral color palette
- No red/green danger colors

---

#### PANEL D — OPPORTUNITY SIGNALS
- Simple visual or list showing:
  - Sectors with rising demand
  - Adjacent roles with similar task profiles
- Caption:
  "These are structural opportunity areas, not guarantees."

#### PANEL E — CONTEXT & ACCESS (TOGGLEABLE)

- Language sensitivity indicator
