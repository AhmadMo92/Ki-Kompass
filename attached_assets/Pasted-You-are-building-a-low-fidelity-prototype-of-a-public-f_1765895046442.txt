You are building a low-fidelity prototype of a public-facing
"AI & Workforce Intelligence Dashboard".

This is NOT a job board and NOT a career advice tool.

The goal of the homepage/dashboard is:
- to calmly explain how AI is reshaping work through tasks and skills
- to reduce fear and confusion
- to help users understand patterns, not predict outcomes

Design a simple, clean dashboard-style homepage with the following principles:
- neutral, non-alarmist tone
- task- and skill-based framing (not job loss)
- clear separation between data, interpretation, and uncertainty
- suitable for a general workforce audience, including migrants

### PAGE STRUCTURE

1. HEADER
- Title: "How AI Is Changing Work — Tasks, Skills, Opportunities"
- Subtitle: "A research-based view on AI, jobs, and labor market change"
- Small disclaimer: "This platform explains patterns and trends. It does not predict individual outcomes."

2. TOP SUMMARY STRIP
A short text block that dynamically summarizes:
- whether AI is mostly augmenting or automating tasks (placeholder)
- whether skill change pressure is low / medium / high (placeholder)
- whether demand context is stable or shifting (placeholder)

Example tone:
"In the selected context, AI is mainly augmenting tasks.
Skill requirements are changing moderately.
Labor demand remains strong in several adjacent roles."

3. MAIN DASHBOARD SECTIONS

SECTION A — TASK TRANSFORMATION
- Simple visual (bar or split chart) showing:
  - Augmented tasks
  - Partially automated tasks
  - Mostly stable human tasks
- Tooltip text explaining:
  "AI affects tasks within jobs, not entire jobs."

SECTION B — SKILL CHANGE DYNAMICS
- Simple line or band chart showing "Skill Change Intensity" over time (placeholder data)
- Explanatory text:
  "This shows how quickly skill requirements evolve, not job loss."

SECTION C — SECTOR CONTEXT
- Bar chart or grid showing AI exposure by sector (placeholder sectors)
- Neutral color palette
- No red/green danger signals

SECTION D — OPPORTUNITY SIGNALS
- Simple list or chart showing:
  - Sectors with rising demand
  - Roles with similar task profiles
- Language must emphasize:
  "Structural opportunities, not guarantees."

4. INTERPRETATION PANEL
A clearly separated box titled:
"What this means — and what it doesn’t"

Include:
- 3 bullet points explaining what the data suggests
- 2 bullet points explaining what the data does NOT mean
- A note on uncertainty and context dependence

5. FOOTER / TRUST ELEMENTS
- "Data sources & research" (placeholders)
- "Methodology & limitations"
- "About this platform"

### DESIGN STYLE
- Calm colors (blues, greys, soft neutrals)
- Plenty of white space
- No urgency language
- No scores, rankings, or predictions

### IMPORTANT
- Use placeholder or dummy data
- Focus on structure, layout, and tone
- Avoid career advice language
- Avoid claims about individual risk
